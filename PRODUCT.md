# PRODUCT.md — Ungovernable

## Register

**Product.** A single-user personal command center. The design serves the data; every screen exists to log something or read a number quickly.

## Users & Purpose

One user (the owner). Used daily, on **phone (vertical)** and **laptop**, often in the evening — a glance-and-log ritual: check net worth and cash, tick off habits, log an expense or a workout set, read the day's signals (weather, stocks, a video).

Primary jobs:
- **Glance**: net worth, cash, this-month spend, total screen time wasted — instantly.
- **Log**: daily habits, expenses, paychecks, workout sets, project notes.
- **Reflect**: trends over months/years, progress toward goals, consistency (commit grid).

The emotional target is *self-accountability without shame* — the product is named **Ungovernable** and the tone is a sharp personal terminal: honest numbers, no corporate gloss, a little bit of edge ("total screen time so I can see how much of my life has been wasted").

## Brand & Personality

Three words: **disciplined, honest, kinetic.**

A personal dashboard that feels like mission-control for one person's life. Dense but legible. Data-forward. Confident dark surface so charts, the commit grid, and the equity meter glow. Warmth and edge come from a single coral/ember accent — not from decoration.

## Anti-references

- Not a SaaS marketing dashboard (no hero-metric template, no gradient-text, no identical card grids).
- Not cream/beige "editorial warmth." Dark, true-neutral surface; warmth lives in the accent only.
- Not playful/rounded fintech. This is a terminal, not a piggy bank.
- Not enterprise BI grey-on-grey. Color is used deliberately for signal (gain vs. loss, done vs. pending, on-track vs. behind).

## Strategic design principles

1. **Numbers first.** The most important value on any surface is the largest, highest-contrast thing.
2. **Signal color.** Green = gain/done/on-track. Red/ember = loss/behind. Coral accent = brand & interactive. Never decorative color.
3. **One-hand mobile.** Everything reachable and tappable on a vertical phone; tables collapse to stacked rows, nav is a hamburger sheet.
4. **Honest framing.** Screen-time "wasted", spending callouts, behind-goal lines — the app tells the truth plainly.
5. **Fast logging.** Every log action (habit, expense, set) is ≤2 taps from the relevant screen.
